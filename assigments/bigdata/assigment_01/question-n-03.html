Digital Earth: Big Earth Data Concept - assignment I

3 Question N° 03

3.1. Describe how Theia produces and distributes Level-2A data, corrected for atmospheric effects thanks to the MAJA software developed in coordination between CNES/CESBIO and DLR.

MACCS (Multi-sensor Atmospheric Correction and Cloud Screening) combined with ATCOR (Atmospheric and Topographic Correction) generated MAJA (MACCS-ATCOR Joint Algorithm) a software developed and maintained by CNES, CESBIO, and DLR. For Sentinel-2 Level-2A, MAJA only covers France, Germany, and other specific requested areas in the world.

To convert TOA to BOA values, MAJA contains modules to estimate the H20 content and correct the atmospheric absorption (See SMAC). Inspired in ATCOR, also correct the influence of the slope and adjacency effect in reflectance values.

For cloud detection, MAJA works with time-series reflectance values and suppose that surface without clouds are stable in time. Consequently, clouds and cloud shadows are detected analyzing changes in the series (using a cloud-free composites). However, this assumption could be a problem in strongly affected by clouds regions like the Peruvian/Brazilian Amazon (See Sanchez et al. 2020).

3.2. Describe the main differences in the production chains compared to the ESA Level-2A data.

The main differences are:

  • Theia use MAJA while ESA use Sen2cor.

  • Theia level 2A use composites (continuously updated) to enhance aerosol detection and cloud detection.

  • ESA produces a pixel classification map (SC) while Theia only generates a cloud mask (cloud, cloud shadow, and cirrus).
